# Demographic Shift Standard

Purpose:
define how the firm describes demographic change over time
prevent overclaiming from noisy or poorly comparable measures
make demographic narrative outputs more consistent
Use When
Use this standard whenever a project summarizes:
race and ethnicity change
age-structure change
education shift
tenure shift
household or population composition change
Do Not Use When
Do not use this standard to turn every small change into a narrative finding. If the data is weak or the geography is unstable, describe the limitation instead.
Approved Rule
Demographic shift framing should:
state the geography clearly
state the time horizon clearly
distinguish absolute change from share change
distinguish current composition from change over time
avoid causal claims unless the project actually supports them
Inputs
approved indicator package
approved trend horizon
approved geography
any relevant comparability caveats
Method Notes
Report both count change and share change when interpretation depends on both.
Keep category definitions stable across compared vintages.
Prefer grouped summaries over excessive category fragmentation in client-facing outputs.
If a category definition shifted materially across time, note that explicitly.
Do not frame ordinary directional change as a social or market conclusion without evidence.
Validation Rules
A demographic shift summary should fail validation if:
the geography is unstated
the time horizon is unstated
share change is reported without denominator context
counts and shares are mixed loosely
the language implies causation without support
Human Review Gates
Escalate when:
the framing could be politically or commercially sensitive
category comparability is weak
a summary could materially affect client interpretation
Common Failure Modes
confusing counts with shares
implying displacement or market causation without evidence
over-reading small percentage changes
ignoring margin of error or category instability

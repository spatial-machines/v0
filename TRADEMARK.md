# Trademark Policy

## The "spatial-machines" name

"spatial-machines" is an unregistered trademark of The spatial-machines Authors. This policy explains how you may and may not use the name.

## What you can do

- **Use the code.** The Apache-2.0 license grants you broad rights to use, modify, and distribute the software.
- **Say you use it.** "Built with spatial-machines" or "Powered by spatial-machines" is fine.
- **Link to the project.** Reference the official repository at `https://github.com/spatial-machines/spatial-machines`.
- **Fork and modify.** You're encouraged to fork and customize the code for your needs.

## What you must do if you fork

- **Use a different name.** If you distribute a modified version, you must rename it. Do not call your fork "spatial-machines" or any confusingly similar name (e.g., "spatial-machine", "spatialmachines", "spatial_machines").
- **Remove branding.** Replace any logos, social preview images, or marketing copy that implies your fork is the official project.
- **Keep attribution.** The Apache-2.0 license requires you to retain the NOTICE file and copyright headers. Please do.

## What you cannot do

- Distribute a modified version under the "spatial-machines" name.
- Use the name in a way that implies endorsement, partnership, or official association with this project.
- Register "spatial-machines" as a trademark, domain name, or social media handle for a competing product.

## Why this policy exists

We want everyone to fork, customize, and build on this project. That's the point. But we also want users to know which version is the official one and which is a community fork. The trademark policy protects that distinction without restricting your freedom to use the code.

## Questions

Open a GitHub issue or contact the maintainer if you're unsure whether your use is covered.
